Consistency in design

Why it's important

Consistency in API design ensures that users and developers can easily understand and integrate with your service. Predictable APIs reduce the learning curve, improve usability, and minimize errors. When APIs behave in a consistent way, users are more likely to adopt them and maintain long-term use.

What it means

  • Use standard HTTP methods (e.g., GET, POST, PUT, DELETE) for their intended purposes.
  • Keep endpoint naming consistent and follow a clear, logical structure.
  • Avoid introducing unnecessary complexity. For example, use clear, plural nouns for resources like `/users` instead of `/user-list`.
