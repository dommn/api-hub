Plan authentication and security

Security must be built into your API from the beginning. This includes how people access your API, how you protect data, and how you prevent misuse.

Why this matters

APIs often expose sensitive data or allow critical actions. Poor security can lead to data breaches, service disruption, and loss of trust.

What to do

  • Decide who can access the API: public, partner organisations, or internal teams only.
  • Choose the right authentication method:
    • API keys – simple but limited control
    • OAuth 2.0 – standard for secure, token-based access
    • Mutual TLS (mTLS) – for trusted systems and internal use
  • Apply the principle of least privilege – only give access to what's needed.
  • Protect all endpoints with HTTPS and validate all inputs.
  • Log access and usage – securely and in compliance with your organisation's data policies.

Work with security and data teams

Involve your organisation’s security, information assurance and data protection leads early in your design process. They can help you:

  • conduct threat modelling
  • review data classification and privacy risks
  • define acceptable use and access control policies

Never expose secrets or credentials in query strings, logs, or error messages.
