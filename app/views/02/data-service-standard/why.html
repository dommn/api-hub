Why we have this standard

Government departments and public sector organisations increasingly need to share and use data across boundaries to deliver joined-up services and improve outcomes for users. To do this efficiently, data services must be designed with openness, interoperability, and reuse in mind.

The Data Service Standard helps digital teams design and build data services—including APIs and shared datasets—that are secure, well-documented, standards-based, and easy to integrate. It encourages collaboration and consistency, reduces duplication, and ensures public data is managed as a strategic asset.

When to use this standard

You should use this standard if you're:

  • building or improving a RESTful API to expose or consume government data
  • designing services that depend on data from other departments
  • working on data integration, publishing, or interoperability
  • providing reference data or master data for wider reuse
  • managing event-driven or real-time data exchange between systems
